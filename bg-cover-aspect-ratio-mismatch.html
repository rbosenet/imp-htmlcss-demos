Understanding the padding-top:100% and background-position:cover and image cropping

1. Why padding-top: 100% on .post-grid__bg-img is used

The image containers use padding-top: 100% on on .post-grid__bg-img to control height.

In CSS, vertical padding percentages are calculated based on the elementâ€™s width, not its height.

So when we write:

padding-top: 100%;

it means:

  • Height becomes equal to width
  • The element always stays 1:1 (square)
  • The square remains responsive on all screen sizes

2. why background image in first post gets cropped but not second post

Each post-grid__bg-img is square . In first post image is not intrinsicly square (that is aspect ratio is not equal to 1:1 so image gets cropped). In second post background image has been edited to be 1:1 or squared and nothing gets cropped
